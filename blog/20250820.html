Incident Report: TCP/443 Resets Between Mainland China and Non-Mainland Networks

Published: August 20 2025

Window: Aug 20, 2025 12:36–13:49 China Standard Time (UTC+8) • 04:36–05:49 UTC

Timeline (CST, UTC+8)

TimeEvent
12:36First reproducible TCP/443 resets observed on cross-border paths; HTTP/80 remains normal.
12:40–13:40Consistent resets during TLS ClientHello; repeated across multiple prefixes/ASNs.
13:49Behavior ceased; TCP/443 resumed normal operation.

Observed Symptoms

  • TCP/443: Connections reset (RST), either post-three-way handshake or during TLS ClientHello/ServerHello exchange.
  • TCP/80: Unaffected; HTTP requests completed normally.
  • Scope: Affected only when the path traversed CN cross-border interconnect; intra-CN and non-CN↔non-CN paths not observed to fail.
  • Packet captures (indicative): no application-layer response; abrupt RST from path or endpoint midpoint.

Measurement Methodology

We validated across multiple vantage points (different CN eyeball ASNs and non-CN destinations), rotating SNI, IPs, and providers.

Reproduce / Test Commands

Replace DEST_IP and HOSTNAME with your targets. Use a CN source and a non-CN destination.

# Quick TCP/443 handshake vs HTTP/80
					  # 443 likely RST during window; 80 OK
					  curl -I --max-time 5 https://HOSTNAME/ --resolve HOSTNAME:443:DEST_IP
					  curl -I --max-time 5 http://HOSTNAME/  --resolve HOSTNAME:80:DEST_IP
					  
					  # OpenSSL handshake verbosity
					  echo | openssl s_client -connect DEST_IP:443 -servername HOSTNAME -brief -tls1_2
					  
					  # TCP connect timing (requires tcptraceroute or hping3)
					  tcptraceroute DEST_IP 443
					  tcptraceroute DEST_IP 80
					  
					  # Packet capture (server or client side as allowed)
					  sudo tcpdump -nni any 'tcp and (port 443 or port 80)'

What Might Explain This? (Hypotheses)

  • Interconnect policy event or transient filtering: Mid-path device injecting RST on port 443 flows, e.g., triggered by TLS ClientHello properties.
  • Misconfiguration / maintenance: Short-lived change affecting cross-border 443 flows only.
  • Not observed: No evidence that TCP/80 or DNS transport (53/UDP) were impacted in the same window.

Note: These are technical hypotheses based on symptoms. We do not assert intent or definitive root cause.

Impact

  • HTTPS availability degraded for CN users accessing non-CN services.
  • Automations relying on TLS/443 (APIs, package repos, updates) intermittently failed.
  • Plain HTTP remained a functional fallback, if enabled.
