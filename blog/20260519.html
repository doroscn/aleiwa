Iraq Nationwide Internet Shutdown During 2026 Examination Period

May–June 2026 — Scheduled Government Internet Restrictions

The Iraqi Government announced a series of nationwide internet shutdowns during the 2026 middle school examination period. The restrictions were implemented as part of efforts to prevent cheating and information leakage during national exams.

Iraq Nationwide Examination Internet Restrictions

According to notifications distributed by multiple Iraqi internet providers, internet connectivity disruptions were ordered directly by government authorities and were beyond the control of local ISPs.

The shutdowns affected mobile internet services, fixed broadband access, and parts of international connectivity across Iraq.

Scheduled Shutdown Dates

The internet shutdowns were scheduled daily from 6:00 AM to 8:00 AM (UTC+3) on the following dates:

  • 20 May 2026
  • 24 May 2026
  • 2 June 2026
  • 4 June 2026
  • 7 June 2026
  • 9 June 2026
  • 11 June 2026
  • 14 June 2026

Network Impact

During the shutdown windows, users across Iraq reported:

  • Loss of mobile data connectivity
  • Severe degradation of broadband services
  • International traffic instability
  • Packet loss and routing interruptions
  • Temporary service outages for cloud applications

Several international services and CDN providers also observed abnormal traffic drops originating from Iraqi networks during the scheduled periods.

Background

Iraq has repeatedly implemented internet shutdowns during national examination periods over recent years. The policy is intended to reduce exam-related fraud, though it has drawn criticism from digital rights groups and businesses affected by the disruptions.

These recurring outages continue to impact online services, financial transactions, education platforms, and international communications across the country.

Operational Considerations

Network operators and CDN providers serving Iraqi users should prepare for recurring traffic instability during government-announced examination periods.

  • Expect sudden traffic drops from Iraqi ASN ranges
  • Monitor BGP path stability during outage windows
  • Prepare failover strategies for regional traffic shifts
  • Consider scheduling maintenance outside blackout periods

Source: ISP customer notifications, network telemetry, public internet monitoring reports
