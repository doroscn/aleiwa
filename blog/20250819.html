Pakistan Internet Outage: Connectivity Slashed to 20%

On the evening of August 19, 2025, Pakistan faced a severe Internet crisis. Connectivity dropped to just 20% of normal levels nationwide, according to monitoring firm NetBlocks. This outage crippled connectivity, banking, and everyday online activity across the country.

Event Overview

NetBlocks documented a significant drop in national connectivity, particularly affecting PTCL backbone infrastructure and cascading disruptions to operators like Ufone, Jazz, Zong, and Nayatel [oai_citation:12‡The Express Tribune](https://tribune.com.pk/story/2562106/internet-outage-hits-pakistan-as-connectivity-drops-to-20?utm_source=chatgpt.com) [oai_citation:13‡Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2612359/pakistan?utm_source=chatgpt.com).

“Metrics show a major disruption to internet connectivity across #Pakistan […]; overall national connectivity is down to 20% of ordinary levels.” — NetBlocks

Possible Causes

Press reports cite heavy monsoon rains and flooding in Karachi — especially near submarine cable landing stations — as a likely cause [oai_citation:14‡黎明网](https://www.dawn.com/news/1932012?utm_source=chatgpt.com). PTCL and other providers confirmed technical service failures, attributing outages to upstream network faults [oai_citation:15‡ProPakistani](https://propakistani.pk/2025/08/19/internet-down-across-pakistan-heres-whats-causing-it/?utm_source=chatgpt.com).

WhenWhat Happened

Impact

  • Business operations and banking services were severely affected, delaying transactions and communication.
  • Millions of users were unable to access the Internet, amplifying public frustration across social media.
  • Digital experts pointed to infrastructure fragility — particularly reliance on single providers and lack of redundancy [oai_citation:16‡The News International](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1337171-pakistan-hit-by-major-internet-outage?utm_source=chatgpt.com).

Reflections & Recommendations

  • Enhance infrastructure resilience by diversifying operators and submarine cable access.
  • Deploy comprehensive monitoring and alert systems across national and cross-border links.
  • Provide transparency during incidents through timely public updates and restoration timelines.
